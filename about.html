David Cordova

Bio

Hi, I’m David. I am a technology and music enthusiast, otaku, web developer, musician, nonfluent polyglot. I have an ability to identify an unimportant percentage of vehicles just by the logo or sound of it’s exhaust. I am driven to analyze objects, ideas, and conversations heuristically for deeper understanding and insight into user interactions.

Web development entered my life during middle school, during an era when it was uncommon to find personal computers in every home. I spent many hours learning from instructional books in the library’s technology section. My dedication, drive, and passion for visual creation found a digital outlet and thus magnified into many of my other forms of artistic expression.

I am enthusiastic in my voyage through the vast and intricate digital universe to attain solutions that are harmonious with user interactions.

2002

First step

My parents bought our first "home desktop" computer.

With the help of HTML books from the library and promotional AOL CD-ROM discs that gave you "free minutes" to connect to the internet, I spent all summer determined to teach myself code.

2003

First creation

For years, my uncle would mail out typed newsletters every 6 months to family members living out of state to keep everyone informed of reunions and holiday gatherings. Using my creativeness, I created a website for the family to read these online and to interact together on the site.

* (August 1, 2003) - Myspace patented it before me

2005

First Promotional

A small church band that I was actively apart of needed help promoting their music and their presence in the area. With determination and creativeness under my belt, I designed and created my first promotional website on the web for the band "The Grupo".

2017

First Career

Over 10+ years in the workforce, I found my way back to Web Development through helping others learn basic coding skills. My journey was set and since then I have earned my associates in Web Development (2019) and soon a Bachelors in Internet and Web Development (2020).
